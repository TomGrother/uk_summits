About Us BETA

SummitStack is a small, independent project built to help hillwalkers track their progress on Britain's best-loved hills — starting with the 189 Welsh Nuttalls and the 214 Lake District Wainwrights. We're hikers ourselves, and built this because we wanted a simple, free way to log climbs, see them on a map, and compare notes with friends.

The site is currently in beta, which means it's still under active development. Expect new features, occasional bugs, and changes as we improve things based on feedback. If something looks wrong or you have an idea, please let us know!

FAQ BETA

What is a Nuttall?
A Nuttall is a mountain in England or Wales over 2,000ft (610m) with a drop of at least 15m on all sides. This site tracks the 189 Nuttalls in Wales.
What is a Wainwright?
A Wainwright is one of the 214 Lake District fells described in Alfred Wainwright's "Pictorial Guides to the Lakeland Fells". This site tracks all 214 of them.
Do I need an account?
Yes — a free account is required to use the map and track which summits you've climbed. Registration just needs a username, email and password.
How do I mark a summit as climbed?
Click a summit marker on the map or a summit in the sidebar list, then use the "Mark as climbed" button in the popup, or tick the checkbox next to its name in the sidebar.
How do I find a specific summit?
Use the search box at the top of the sidebar to filter summits by name. Matching summits' regions will expand automatically.
What are badges?
Badges are awarded for milestones (e.g. your first summit, 10, 25, 50, 100, 200 and completing every summit on the site) and for completing every summit in a region. View yours from the Badges menu.
The site is in beta — what does that mean?
It means we're still actively building and refining SummitStack. Some features may change, and you might spot the occasional bug. Thanks for bearing with us!
